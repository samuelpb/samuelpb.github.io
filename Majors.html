Pre-Med vs Comp-Sci

Why I Switched Majors

  • Sam Beluch
  • College

In high school, I was easily a STEM based student. Through AP in math, biology, chemistry, and physics, I had AP credit for most of my freshan year at college. During the summer post junior and senior year, I participated in research a Rowan on protein interactions within the cell in regards to cell apoptosis. And, I had my mind set: I would be in med-school in a few years, on my way to being a surgeon.

Headed into college, I was between two: a pre-medicine research based progam at Ohio State and the Honors College at Rutgers' University in New Brunswick. Ultimately, the Presidential Scholarship at Rutgers made my decsion for me, as the cost to go to Ohio was not worth it. Freshman year I was in a research class, and with my experience from Rowan I enjoyed the class, even helping to tutor a few friends in it. At the same time, I entered Honors Organic Chemistry, where things took a turn.

Now, I know people would think this is a typical "Organic Chem is the weed-out class" case. However, it began a realization for me that quickly led to me switching majors. In fact, by mid-fall of my sophomore year I had fully changed my major. I made it through Organic Chem, and even into Biochem, where I eventually withdrew.

Back in high school, I had a friend, Eddy Yeung, in an introductory computer science class. Edy knew my passion for math and science, and taught me a little bit to pique my intrest. By the end of the year, I was helping him think of solutions and writing some small programs for myself for fun. I should have realized at the time, but instead I was so set on being pre-med that I let my passion escape from under me. The way of thinking, the logic, the feeling when a code works correctly, the abstractness, everything. It was me using my mind the way it was wired best.

Now at Rutgers, in the spring of my freshman year, alongside Organic Chem, as an elective I took Intro to Computer Science. I found myself focusing on the projects and homeworks for this class, even though I knew most of the material thanks to Eddy, rather than Organic. And for the sole reason that I enjoyed it more.

Pre-med is very memorization based. Don't get me wrong, there is plenty of analysis and application, and in no way is it an easy major. But, the memorization behind the classes I took was just not enjoyable for me. I did not learn like that, but through application instead. For this reason, I had enjoyed research---hands on application of science with results that I could see and interpret. But, memorizing amino acids, the krebs cycle, and different reagents and products to produce Ketones was not my style.

My professors from when I did research mentioned to me that once I got through Orgo things would be better. In biochem, there was supposed to be more application, but instead I needed to know every last detail about every last step of glycolosis. So I withdrew, adn focused on my Data Structures class as a committed computer science major.

Looking back, this was one of the best decisions I made academically. I thoroughly enjoy coding and the way of thinking behind it, and it has led me to amazing career opportunities through my first internship at JP Morgan and first job at Amazon. I also received math and cognitive science minors, and enjoyed my studies rather than loathing the notecards that come with memorization.

In the end, I respect everyone going through pre-med and medical school. It was not for me, but it may be for you. I was lucky, and with my AP credits I was able to still finish in four years even though I switched majors. I was a star STEM student, who was supposed to be a doctor because that is what star STEM students do. But, I enjoyed another way of thinking and another passion, and I am so happy I made the switch. A switch that I think will become more and more popular as schooling prices rise, while demand and salaries rise for computer science majors.
